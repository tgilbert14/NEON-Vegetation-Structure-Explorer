What you can do

The Size Lab

The flagship: every plant as a dot in size × height space (DBH for trees, basal diameter for shrubs), split into four size-shapes. Tap a dot to pin its card, drag & resize, then download the chart with the cards baked in.

Stand structure

The size-class distribution (the forester's reverse-J for trees, basal classes for shrubs), the height profile, and per-hectare basal area, stem density, and QMD — mean ± SE across plots.

Growth & mortality

Annualised diameter growth between remeasurements, the fastest growers, and the live / standing-dead / lost-track split — decreases kept and flagged, not deleted.

Champions

The record-holders — biggest, tallest, fastest-growing, longest-tracked — on a re-sortable leaderboard and podium. Tap one to open its career.

Plant Career

Open any tree or shrub for its growth trajectory, size-for-species percentile, QC flags, a shareable plant card (PNG), and the raw per-bout data (CSV).

Compare, export, report

Put two stands head-to-head, download the full tidy dataset + codebook (CSV/zip), or a one-page stand report PDF.

Honest statistics

Every view states its method, on the chart:

  • Snapshot, not pooled — stand metrics use each plant's latest measurement; one remeasured five times is counted once. Growth is the explicit multi-bout metric, annualised between visits.
  • The right size measure per biome — forests are sized by tree DBH; desert shrubs are too short for a breast-height diameter, so they're sized by basal stem diameter (what NEON records for ~96–99% of desert stems). The app classifies each site by basal-area dominance and adapts.
  • Basal area, not biomass — above-ground biomass needs an allometric model whose error compounds, so it is deliberately omitted; directly-measured basal area is the honest stand measure.
  • Indices, not an inventory — per-hectare basal area and density are scaled from the sampled plots (mean ± SE across plots, the sampling unit), not a wall-to-wall census.
  • Decreases are real, kept and flagged — diameter shrinkage between visits (bark sloughing, drought, a changed measurement height) is verified, not deleted. Live/dead is a snapshot ratio, and lost-tag/removed stems are split out from biological mortality.
